# Scientific English Editor

A simple skill for editing scientific and technical English written by non-native English speakers.

This skill focuses on language only, such as grammar, academic tone, clarity, conciseness, and natural English expression. It does not check whether your formulas, experiments, figures, tables, or scientific conclusions are correct.

## Recommended Input

For the best result, please provide your text as:

1. **LaTeX**
2. **Plain text**

These formats are easier to edit and easier for you to copy back into your manuscript.

Word, PDF, or screenshot inputs can also be used, but they are not recommended as the first choice. They may require extra layout handling, and some formatting may be harder to preserve.

## How to Use

Paste the scientific English text you want to edit.

For example, you can provide:

- an abstract
- one paragraph from the introduction
- a method description
- a results paragraph
- a figure caption
- a table caption
- LaTeX text from your paper

The skill will usually provide:

1. sentence-by-sentence revision notes
2. a full revised version that you can copy

## Suggested Workflow

Do not paste the whole paper at once unless necessary.

It is better to edit one paragraph or one section at a time. This makes the revision easier to check and helps you understand your language problems more clearly.

A good workflow is:

1. Paste one paragraph.
2. Read the revision notes.
3. Compare the original and revised versions.
4. Copy the revised text back to your paper.
5. Continue with the next paragraph.

This can reduce your checking burden and help you improve your academic English over time.

## Notes

- The revised text is normally written in academic English.
- American English is used by default unless you request another style.
- The skill tries to preserve your original format.
- For LaTeX input, LaTeX commands, equations, citations, labels, and environments should be kept as much as possible.
- For plain text input, the original plain-text style should be kept as much as possible.
